National Parks

With 10 enchanting national parks, Uganda is a sanctuary of life, culture, and color a destination where the Big Five, golden sunsets, and cultural soul combine in one unforgettable journey. Let’s take you there one wonder at a time. *1. Murchison Falls National Park* The Nile’s Mighty Roar Meets Africa’s Wild Heart Uganda’s largest and oldest national park, Murchison is where the Nile explodes through a narrow gorge, tumbling 45 meters into thunder and mist. Big Five: Lions, Elephants, Buffaloes, Leopards (Rhino nearby at Ziwa Sanctuary) Activities: • Sunrise & sunset game drives on the savannah • Boat cruise to the base of the falls • Top-of-the-falls hike • Chimp tracking in Budongo Forest • Birdwatching along the Nile Highlight: Watch elephants drink at the riverbank as the sun sets golden behind the wild horizon. *2* . *Queen Elizabeth National Park* Gifted by Craters, Cats, and the Channel of Life This is Uganda’s most iconic safari park a mosaic of grassland, wetlands, and crater lakes. Big Five: Lions (including tree-climbing), Elephants, Buffaloes, Leopards Activities: • Game drives through Kasenyi Plains • Boat safari along the hippo-rich Kazinga Channel • Explore explosion crater lakes • Chimpanzee tracking in Kyambura Gorge • Birdwatching (over 600 species) Highlight: Ishasha’s legendary tree-climbing lions lounging in fig trees at golden hour. *3.Kidepo Valley National Park* The Wild North – Remote, Raw, and Breathtaking Africa untouched Kidepo is Uganda’s hidden Eden, framed by rugged mountains and endless plains. Big Five: Lions, Elephants, Buffaloes, Leopards Activities: • Game drives in Narus and Kidepo Valleys • Cultural visits to the Ik and Karamojong tribes • Birdwatching among dramatic scenery • Nature walks in the valley floor *Highlight* : Sunset over the Morungole Mountains as lions prowl the golden grasslands. *4.Lake Mburo National Park* The Whispering Wild – Where You Walk with Zebras Close to Kampala, this compact park is alive with elegance and movement. Big Five: Leopards, Buffaloes *Activities* • Guided walking safaris • Horseback and cycling safaris • Boat cruise on Lake Mburo • Birdwatching and cultural encounters *Highlight* : A sunrise walk among grazing zebras, wrapped in mist and silence. *5* . *Bwindi Impenetrable Forest National Park* In the Realm of Giants Where Eyes Meet the Soul Home to nearly half the world’s mountain gorillas, this ancient forest breathes mystery and majesty. *Activities* : • Gorilla trekking • Batwa cultural experience • Forest nature walks and birdwatching *Highlight* : A face-to-face moment with a silverback gorilla in the emerald shadows. *6* . *Kibale Forest National Park* The Chimpanzee Capital where Forests Speak in Echoes Lush, dense, and magical Kibale is Africa’s finest place to see chimpanzees in the wild. *Activities* • Chimpanzee tracking • Bigodi Wetland nature walks • Birdwatching • Cultural experiences with Batooro communities *Highlight* : A morning trek through tangled vines to hear the laughter of chimps echoing above. *7. Rwenzori Mountains National Park* The Mountains of the Moon Snow on the Equator Mythical and magnificent, the Rwenzoris offer alpine adventures in equatorial Africa. *Activities* : • Multi-day glacier treks • Birdwatching and high-altitude hiking • Cultural trails in nearby Bakonzo communities *Highlight* : The moonlight glow on snow-capped peaks. *8. Bwindi Impenetrable Forest National Park* Where Silver Meets Gold Gorillas and Golden Monkeys Tucked in the Virunga ranges, this sacred land shelters rare mountain gorillas and playful golden monkeys. *Activities* • Gorilla trekking • Golden monkey tracking • Volcano hiking (Mt. Sabinyo, Gahinga, Muhavura) • Batwa trail experience *Highlight*: Watching golden monkeys dance through bamboo as the volcanoes rise behind. *9. Mount Elgon National Park* The Ancient Giant Waterfalls, Peaks & Peace Once taller than Kilimanjaro, Elgon is a calm mountain wilderness of caves, forests, and cliffs. *Activities* • Sipi Falls hikes • Birding and nature walks • Cave visits and coffee tours • Mountain trekking *Highlight*: A sunrise view over Sipi Falls as the land below lights up like fire. *10. Semuliki National Park* It's Uganda’s Hidden Eden Steam, Forest, and Feathered Glory Part of the Congo basin, Semuliki is one of the most biodiverse spots in Africa. *Activities* • Visit Sempaya hot springs • Forest walks to spot rare primates • Birdwatching paradise • Cultural encounters with the Bamaga people *Highlight*: Standing at the bubbling hot springs, surrounded by forest spirits and birdsong. Let us craft your safari with heart, soul, and stories. Uganda is waiting come feel her wild beauty.
